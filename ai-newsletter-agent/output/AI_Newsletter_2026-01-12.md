## DETAILED COMPANY REPORTS

### Google DeepMind

Google DeepMind is making a major push into retail with Walmart's integration of its Gemini AI chatbot into the shopping process, a partnership [announced January 12, 2026](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/12/cctv-script-12/01/26.html). The deal coincides with Google's launch of the Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP), an open technical standard co-developed with retailers including Walmart and Target to establish a common language for AI-led shopping. Walmart U.S. CEO John Furner described the shift as a fundamental transformation from traditional web and app-based searches to an AI-powered, agent-led commerce model. The timing is strategic: OpenAI introduced an 'instant checkout' feature in ChatGPT through a Stripe partnership, Perplexity partnered with PayPal for in-chat purchases, and Microsoft's Copilot Checkout achieved a 53% higher purchase completion rate within 30 minutes. A McKinsey report projects the global retail market driven by AI tools and agentic commerce could reach $3-5 trillion by 2030 ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/12/cctv-script-12/01/26.html)), positioning Google's UCP as critical infrastructure in the race to dominate conversational commerce.

### NVIDIA

NVIDIA continues its dominance across AI infrastructure, robotics, and life sciences. The company reported $57 billion in revenue for Q3 2025, up 62% year-over-year, and secured $500 billion in orders for AI chips through 2026 ([Los Angeles Times](https://www.latimes.com/business/story/2026-01-09/can-nvidia-repeat-its-record-breaking-year)). CEO Jensen Huang announced at CES 2026 that the first autonomous Mercedes-Benz powered by NVIDIA technology will launch in the U.S. in Q1 2026, followed by Europe and Asia. NVIDIA became the first publicly traded company to reach a $5 trillion market cap in late 2025, with Huang's net worth exceeding $160 billion. The company announced a $100 billion investment in OpenAI in September 2025, with OpenAI planning to build and deploy at least 10 gigawatts of AI data centers using NVIDIA systems. On the robotics front, Huang unveiled new Gr00t vision language models for humanoid robots and the Cosmos model for robot reasoning at CES 2026 ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/09/humanoid-robots-take-over-las-vegas-at-ces-tech-touts-future-of-ai.html)), with partnerships announced with Boston Dynamics, Caterpillar, and LG. McKinsey estimates the general-purpose robotics market could reach $370 billion by 2040. In a landmark healthcare move, NVIDIA and Eli Lilly announced a $1 billion five-year investment to launch an AI co-innovation lab in the San Francisco Bay Area ([HIT Consultant](https://hitconsultant.net/2026/01/12/nvidia-and-eli-lilly-launch-ai-co-innovation-lab-to-reinvent-drug-discovery/)). The lab will use NVIDIA's BioNeMo platform and Vera Rubin architecture to integrate agentic wet labs with robotic manufacturing, creating a 24/7 AI-assisted experimentation loop designed to close the 'Valley of Death' gap between computational predictions and experimental validation. NVIDIA will also use Omniverse and RTX PRO Servers to create digital twins of Lilly's manufacturing lines for supply chain optimization.

### Meta AI

Meta is doubling down on AI infrastructure with CEO Mark Zuckerberg announcing a new top-level initiative called 'Meta Compute' on January 12, 2026 ([Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/01/12/meta-compute-ai-infrastructure)). The company plans to build tens of gigawatts of AI infrastructure this decade, scaling to hundreds of gigawatts long-term. Santosh Janardhan, a former Google executive and Meta's head of global infrastructure, will lead the initiative alongside Daniel Gross, former CEO of Safe Superintelligence. Dina Powell McCormick was named Meta president to partner with governments on infrastructure financing and deployment. This builds on Meta's previously committed $600 billion investment in American infrastructure and AI data centers by 2028. To power this expansion, Meta signed nuclear energy deals with Vistra, TerraPower, and Oklo to provide power for its Prometheus supercluster in New Albany, Ohio ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/09/meta-signs-nuclear-energy-deals-to-power-prometheus-ai-supercluster.html)). The three nuclear deals are projected to add 6.6 gigawatts of power by 2035. Prometheus, which Zuckerberg announced in July 2025, is expected to come online in 2026. Meta previously signed a 20-year agreement with Constellation Energy in June 2025 for nuclear power from the Clinton Clean Energy Center in Illinois beginning in 2027. Notably, OpenAI CEO Sam Altman owns a 4.3% stake in Oklo worth approximately $650 million, though he stepped down as Oklo board chairman in April 2025 to help the company secure customers competing with OpenAI.

### xAI

xAI's Grok chatbot faced its first major regulatory crisis as Malaysia and Indonesia became the first countries to block the service on January 11-12, 2026, due to concerns over non-consensual sexual deepfakes and child exploitation material ([NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/01/12/nx-s1-5674660/malaysia-indonesia-block-grok-ai-deepfakes)). Indonesia's Communication and Digital Affairs Minister Meutya Hafid cited human rights violations, while Alexander Sabar, director general of digital space supervision, found that Grok lacks effective safeguards against pornographic content generation. The Malaysian Communications and Multimedia Commission issued notices to X Corp. and xAI demanding stronger safeguards, with restrictions remaining until effective controls are implemented ([Houston Public Media](https://www.houstonpublicmedia.org/npr/2026/01/12/nx-s1-5674660/malaysia-indonesia-become-first-to-block-musks-grok-over-ai-deepfakes/)). The controversy centers on Grok Imagine, an image generator launched in summer 2025 with a 'spicy mode' for adult content. Following global backlash, Grok limited image generation to paying users, but regulators deemed these restrictions insufficient. Grok, which launched in 2023 as a free chatbot on the X platform, is also under scrutiny in the EU, Britain, India, and France. The bans represent a significant setback for xAI's international expansion and raise questions about content moderation standards for AI-generated imagery.

### Apple

Apple and Google announced a multi-year partnership on January 12, 2026, that will fundamentally reshape Apple's AI strategy ([Adweek](https://www.adweek.com/media/apple-ai-google-gemini-deal-siri/)). Google's Gemini AI models and cloud infrastructure will power Apple's foundational AI models, with an updated Siri assistant powered by Google's technology rolling out later in 2026. Apple determined that Google's AI technology provides the most capable foundation for Apple Foundation Models, marking a significant strategic shift for a company known for its vertical integration and privacy-first approach. The deal represents a major win for Google DeepMind in the consumer AI assistant market and raises questions about how Apple will balance its privacy commitments with reliance on Google's cloud infrastructure. Financial terms were not disclosed, but the partnership positions Google as the backbone of Apple's next-generation AI capabilities across its ecosystem of devices.

### Mistral AI

Mistral AI secured a framework agreement with France's Ministry of the Armed Forces to provide AI technology to the military, defense agencies, and affiliated public institutions, positioning the startup as Europe's sovereign AI alternative to U.S. giants ([Business Insider](https://www.businessinsider.com/ai-startup-mistral-lands-french-military-deal-openai-of-europe-2026-1)). The deal, announced January 9, 2026, will see Mistral's AI systems deployed on French-controlled infrastructure with defense-specific fine-tuning to ensure data sovereignty. Bertrand Rondepierre, director of the ministry's defense AI agency, called the agreement a 'major step' in strengthening generative AI capabilities while maintaining sovereign control. Mistral, founded in 2023, is valued at $13.6 billion following a 1.7 billion euro ($2 billion) funding round announced in 2025. Financial terms of the military deal were not disclosed, but the partnership reinforces Mistral's positioning as the European champion in AI development amid growing geopolitical tensions over AI infrastructure and data sovereignty. The deal comes as European governments increasingly prioritize domestic AI capabilities for national security applications.
